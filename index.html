🎯 Best Practices for Prompt Engineering

Be Specific and Clear

Provide concrete details rather than vague requests. Instead of "write something about marketing," specify "write a 500-word blog post about email marketing best practices for small businesses."

Provide Context

Include relevant background information, target audience, purpose, and constraints. Context helps Claude tailor its response to your specific situation.

Use Examples

When possible, include examples of what you're looking for. Examples clarify your expectations and help Claude match your desired style and format.

Set Clear Boundaries

Specify what you don't want as well as what you do want. Include word limits, style restrictions, or content guidelines to avoid unwanted outputs.

Iterate and Improve

Use the results to refine your prompts. Save successful prompts to your history and build upon what works for your specific use cases.

Test Different Approaches

Experiment with different roles, tones, and detail levels to find what works best for different types of tasks and content.

❌ Common Mistakes to Avoid

🚫

Being Too Vague

Avoid generic requests like "help me with writing." Be specific about what type of writing, purpose, audience, and format you need.

🚫

Overloading with Information

While detail is good, avoid overwhelming Claude with too many competing requirements. Focus on the most important aspects.

🚫

Ignoring Output Format

Don't forget to specify how you want the information structured. The format significantly affects usability of the response.

🚫

Not Setting the Right Tone

Mismatched tone can make responses unsuitable for your context. Match the tone to your audience and purpose.
